Staircase Interior Fill — Blue fills gaps between same-color walls

🔬 Algorithm: Staircase Interior Fill

Discovery

Colored shapes form nested staircase/step patterns. Blue (color 1) is placed approximately in the input but needs to be redistributed to correctly fill the staircase interior. The total number of blue cells is conserved between input and output.

Classification of Empty Cells

TYPE A (Closed Gaps): An empty cell where the nearest non-bg cell to the LEFT and the nearest to the RIGHT are the same color. These are gaps between same-color staircase walls that always get filled.
TYPE B (Open Extensions): An empty cell with a non-bg cell to its LEFT but either no cell to the right or a different color. These extend the fill toward the open side of the staircase.

Fill Order

Step 1: Remove all blue cells. Count them = N.
Step 2: Fill ALL Type A cells (closed gaps always filled).
Step 3: Fill Type B cells from bottom to top until total = N.
