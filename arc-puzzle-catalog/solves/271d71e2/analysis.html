📐 Transformation Rule

Box Slide & Fill: Black-bordered rectangles with grey/orange interiors slide toward paired maroon rail lines.

Movement: distance = min(gap_between_maroon_lines, remaining_grey_cells)

Orange Fill: Each step of movement adds exactly 1 orange cell, filling perpendicular to movement:

  • UP → row-by-row, top→bottom, left→right
  • DOWN → row-by-row, bottom→top, right→left
  • LEFT → column-by-column, left→right, bottom→top
  • RIGHT → column-by-column, right→left, top→bottom

Fully-orange boxes don't move (movement = 0).

Maroon rails: Near maroon slides with the box; far maroon stays fixed. If they meet, they merge.

🔬 Algorithm Details

Step 1: Find Boxes

Detect black-bordered (0) rectangles via flood fill. Interior cells are grey (5) or orange (7).

Step 2: Find Maroon Rails

Each box has maroon (9) on exactly one side. Scan that direction for a second maroon line → these form a "near" and "far" rail pair.

Step 3: Calculate Movement

movement = min(distance_between_rails, remaining_grey_cells)

If all grey cells would be filled before reaching the far rail, the box stops early (fully filled).

Step 4: Fill Interior

Each step adds 1 orange cell. The fill sweeps perpendicular to the movement direction, starting from the movement-side corner. The sweep direction within each slice is the counter-clockwise rotation of the sweep axis.

Step 5: Rebuild Grid

Old box positions → background (6). Place boxes at new positions with updated interiors. Place maroon rails (far stays, near slides with box or merges).
